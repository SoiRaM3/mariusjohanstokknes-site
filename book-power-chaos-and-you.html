THE BOOK

An examination of invisible power, internalized obedience, algorithms, conformity, spectacle, and the structures shaping modern behaviour precisely because they no longer look like control.

Power works most efficiently when it does not look like command. It shapes visibility, reward, language, participation, reputation, and the limits of what a person can imagine without appearing unreasonable.

The book follows authority as it moves inward: from institutions and spectacle into self-monitoring, algorithmic participation, social performance, and the obedient mirror.

FROM THE CAVE OF SCREENS

Read the opening

Selected text from the published edition

The cave wasn’t destroyed. It went wireless.

You live in illusions. You breathe them. You defend them. You call them freedom.

Plato warned you about the cave, centuries before Instagram, TikTok, or Twitter existed. Humanity chained, facing shadows, mistaking them for reality. You read the story, maybe even quoted it in class. You thought it was about other people—the ignorant, the uneducated, the masses. But the truth is uncomfortable: you never left the cave.

You just traded stone walls for glass screens, flickering lights for notifications, shadows for algorithms.

The cave hums. It scrolls. It whispers. It has learned to give you personalized shadows, tailored to your hunger, calibrated to your fears, tuned to your desires.

It knows you better than you know yourself. And you let it.

— Power, Chaos & YouBack to book details ↑
