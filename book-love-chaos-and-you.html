THE BOOK

An examination of intimacy, projection, loneliness, comparison, emotional labour, digital performance, and the moment market logic enters the heart.

Love enters a culture already organized by comparison, scarcity, performance, therapy language, market logic, and infinite visible alternatives. Intimacy is asked to provide safety while both people remain economically and neurologically under siege.

The book treats love as co-regulation between unstable systems: tenderness, projection, labour, loneliness, desire, resentment, repair, and grief moving through two bodies that cannot fully know each other.

FROM AFTER THE FIRE

Read the opening

Selected text from the published edition

You reach a point in life where everything starts to sound like a TED Talk. Everyone has advice, nobody has peace. We’ve turned “healing” into a side hustle, and “communication” into warfare.

Somewhere between self-awareness and self-obsession, we forgot how to simply be human.

That’s what this book is about—what’s left when all the slogans stop working.

It’s not a guide. It’s not therapy. It’s not even hopeful in the way people mean when they say that word.

The point isn’t to convince you. It’s to stand beside you for a while, in that strange, half-lit space where logic breaks and emotion finally tells the truth.

It was written in that same fatigue. Not out of wisdom. Out of survival. Out of the desperate need to find something worth believing in after belief itself started to sound like marketing.

— Love, Chaos & YouBack to book details ↑
