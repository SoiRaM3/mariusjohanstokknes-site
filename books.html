The Chaos Series

Begin with the pressure you recognize.

Each volume can be read independently. Together, the books form an anatomy of the human organism living inside systems it built, inherited, internalized, and frequently mistakes for reality itself.

Civilization, Chaos & You

A social-philosophical autopsy of roads, borders, bureaucracy, markets, inherited trauma, technological systems, and the frightened animal hiding beneath the infrastructure.

This book will not fix civilization.

Entropy, Chaos & You

A study of impermanence, maintenance, exhaustion, collapse, and the cost of resisting the direction every structure eventually follows.

This book will not stop the decay.

Faith, Chaos & You

A confrontation with belief, ritual, doubt, spiritual hunger, technology, metrics, and the strange altars human beings keep building after insisting they no longer worship anything.

This book will not save your faith.

Power, Chaos & You

An examination of invisible power, internalized obedience, algorithms, conformity, spectacle, and the structures shaping modern behavior precisely because they no longer look like control.

This book will not save you.

Identity, Chaos & You

A psychological and philosophical autopsy of masks, memory, trauma, reinforcement, social performance, neurodivergence, and the unstable machinery used to assemble the self.

This book will not tell you who you are.

Information, Chaos & You

A study of overload, media saturation, attention erosion, short-form content, selective truth, algorithms, and the collapse of proportion.

This book will not teach you which information is correct.

Love, Chaos & You

An examination of intimacy, projection, loneliness, comparison, emotional labor, digital performance, and the moment market logic enters the heart.

This book will not save your relationship.

Philosophy, Chaos & You

A descent into consciousness, meaning, contradiction, recursive thought, certainty, and the conceptual cages human beings build so chaos has somewhere to pace.

This book will not save what is left of your sanity.

Time, Chaos & You

A philosophical study of urgency, memory, aging, productivity, rest, deadlines, and the quiet authority of the schedule.

This book will not save your schedule.

Reality, Chaos & You

An examination of perception, interpretation, biology, trauma, addiction, cognitive limits, and the fact that insight does not always create leverage.

This book will not make your reality — or help you escape it.

Standalone Philosophical Works

The machinery continues outside the series.

BUY THIS BOOK.

An Autopsy of Consciousness

A philosophical autopsy of consciousness, identity, meaning, and time. Not a guide. Not a promise of transformation. A confrontation with the possibility that awareness does not save you from existence. It merely makes existence harder to lie about.

The Lie That Someone Knows

On Conspiracy, Certainty, and Exhaustion

A short examination of conspiracy thinking, uncertainty, distrust, and the emotional need to believe that somebody is still steering the machine. Not a debunking manual. An anatomy of the point where exhaustion begins pretending to be insight.

Fiction

Systems become stories.

Autonomy Line

A Novella

Debt, quantified labor, compromised autonomy, and quiet trade-offs hidden inside systems designed to feel convenient. In a world where hours can be sold, logged, and extracted while the body sleeps, Emil Isaksson begins noticing fractures in the machinery surrounding his life.

“We were never just paying interest. We were paying ourselves away.”

The Algorithm of Submission

A Novella

A dark speculative novella about artificial intelligence, dependency, institutional control, and the point where optimization begins shaping reality more efficiently than the people supposedly governing it.

“In a world where freedom is an illusion, submission becomes the only path to survival.”

The Stokknes Chaos Theory Compendium

The structural foundation beneath the wider body of work.

A mythic-analytical framework connecting cognition, systems, perception, identity, time, power, meaning, and collapse.

The Compendium organizes the wider project across ten domains and ten named models: a public map of the machinery behind the books.
