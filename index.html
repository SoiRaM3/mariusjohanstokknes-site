READ THE WRITING

From The Quiet Floor

The first thing Maeva noticed when she stepped out of Málaga María Zambrano station was the heat. Not warmth. Heat.

It came up from the pavement and down from the white sky and sideways from the buses breathing diesel at the curb. It touched her face with the confidence of a stranger who had already decided they knew her.

Later, Málaga passed in fragments through the window: palm trees, apartment blocks, a pharmacy cross blinking green, a woman carrying bread, a dog sleeping under a parked scooter, balconies full of laundry moving slightly in the hot air.

It was beautiful in the wrong way. Too bright to be gentle. Too alive to be safe.
