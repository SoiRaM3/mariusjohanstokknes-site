Books

Marius Johan Stokknes

The books form a connected body of work examining perception, power, identity, information, faith, intimacy, time, and the systems people mistake for reality. Each volume approaches a different pressure point of modern life. Together, they form a philosophical dissection of the human being inside the machinery of the world it has built.

The Chaos Series

Power, Chaos & You

A confrontation with invisible power, internalized obedience, social fear, spectacle, algorithms, conformity, and the quiet structures shaping modern behavior. The book examines how control becomes strongest when it no longer looks like control, when people begin carrying the prison inside themselves and mistake participation for freedom.

Love, Chaos & You

An examination of intimacy in an age of endless comparison, emotional performance, digital connection, projection, loneliness, and exhaustion. The book explores what happens when the market enters the heart, when affection becomes measurable, availability becomes labor, and people begin confusing being seen with being known.

Time, Chaos & You

A philosophical study of urgency, memory, aging, productivity, rest, deadlines, and the illusion of ordered experience. The book looks at how time becomes more than measurement: a quiet authority shaping guilt, ambition, fatigue, and the strange modern belief that every moment must justify itself.

Entropy, Chaos & You

A descent into disorder, fragmentation, decay, collapse, and the slow erosion of coherence. The book approaches entropy not only as a physical principle, but as something lived through the body, the mind, relationships, institutions, and every structure people build in the hope that something might remain stable.

Reality, Chaos & You

An examination of perception, interpretation, biology, trauma, addiction, cognitive limits, and the conditions people cannot simply think their way out of. The book confronts the uncomfortable fact that understanding does not always create leverage, that reality does not respond, and that the organism still has limits no matter how clearly the machinery is seen.

Identity, Chaos & You

A psychological and philosophical autopsy of selfhood, masks, memory, trauma, algorithms, dopamine loops, social performance, neurodivergence, and internal conflict. The book rejects the idea of a stable, singular self and instead examines identity as a survival strategy: a negotiated system built from pressure, habit, fear, and the stories used to hold the whole structure together.

Information, Chaos & You

A study of information overload, media saturation, attention erosion, short-form content, misinformation, selective truth, language, context, algorithms, and the collapse of proportion. The book asks how humanity became more informed than ever while often becoming less capable of deciding what deserves belief, what deserves attention, and what any of it actually means.

Civilization, Chaos & You

An examination of institutions, infrastructure, collective dependency, order, spectacle, memory, collapse, and the systems holding modern life together. The book approaches civilization not as the opposite of chaos, but as one of the ways chaos temporarily organizes itself before people begin mistaking the arrangement for permanence.

Faith, Chaos & You

A philosophical examination of belief after traditional certainty begins to weaken. The book explores ritual, surrender, meaning, technology, metrics, productivity, performance, and the persistent human need to feel witnessed by something larger than the self. It asks whether faith disappeared, or simply adapted to a world of screens, systems, and constant measurement.

Related Works

The Lie That Someone Knows

A book about conspiracy, hidden order, certainty, exhaustion, and the human need to believe that someone, somewhere, understands what is happening. When reality becomes too complicated to tolerate, the idea of a secret plan may feel more comforting than the possibility that chaos is ordinary, distributed, and often unmanaged.
